## 1. Purpose

This document defines the end-to-end process by which Apex Mutual evaluates, decides, and communicates the outcome of a residential property insurance claim. It governs every claim from the moment a First Notice of Loss is received until the case is closed, payment is issued, and the claimant has been notified of the decision.

The process exists to ensure that:

- Coverage decisions are consistent, accurate, and traceable to specific policy language.
- Payout amounts respect the contractual limits, sublimits, deductibles, and endorsements.
- Claimants are treated fairly, communicated with promptly, and provided clear data and appeal rights.
- Suspicious or ambiguous claims are escalated to experienced reviewers rather than auto-decided.
- A complete audit record is preserved for every decision.

---

## 2. Scope

### 2.1 In scope

- First-party residential property claims under HO-3 ("Special Form") homeowner policies.
- Coverages A–D: Dwelling, Other Structures, Personal Property, Loss of Use.
- Claims filed by the named insured or an authorised representative.
- Adjudication outcomes: full approval, partial approval, denial.

### 2.2 Out of scope

- Coverage E (Personal Liability) and Coverage F (Medical Payments).
- Commercial property claims; condominium (HO-6) claims; tenant (HO-4) claims.
- Subrogation and recovery actions following settlement.
- Litigation and appraisal proceedings (these are handled by separate procedures and may follow a denial).

## 3. Roles and responsibilities

| Role                              | Responsibilities                                                                                                                                  |
| --------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Claimant (Insured)**            | Submits the First Notice of Loss; provides supporting documentation; receives all communications; retains the right to appeal.                    |
| **Claims Department**             | Receives and logs the claim; verifies completeness; performs eligibility and coverage analysis; calculates the payout; communicates the decision. |

| **Compliance**                    | Reviews the audit record to confirm that decisions are properly documented and policy language is correctly cited.                                |

## 4. Documents and reference data

### 4.1 Required claim documents

Every claim must be supported by three documents:

| Document                         | Source                        | Purpose                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                |
| -------------------------------- | ----------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **First Notice of Loss (FNOL)**  | Claimant                      | Reports the incident, identifies the claimant and the insured property, describes the damage, lists each damaged item with an estimated cost, and states the total claimed amount.                                                                                     |
| **Insurance Policy**             | Apex Mutual records           | Defines the coverage, limits and sublimits, deductible, exclusions, endorsements, and special conditions in force at the time of the loss.                                                                                                                             |
| **Professional Incident Report** | Independent licensed assessor | Documents the assessor's on-site observations, determines the cause of loss, describes the full scope of damage, and provides an independent repair estimate. Note: this document is optional with initial FNOL submission, but is mandatory for final claim decision. |

A claim cannot progress past **Eligibility Analysis** until all three documents are on file.

### 4.2 Reference data

| Reference                | Purpose                                                                                                                                                                                      |
| ------------------------ | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Prior claims history** | All prior claims filed by the same claimant against the same or any other property. Used to spot duplicate claims, repeated incident types, frequency patterns, or escalating claim amounts. |
| **Policy schedule**      | The detailed coverage limits, sublimits, deductible amounts, and active endorsements. Embedded in the Insurance Policy document.                                                             |

### 4.3 Outputs

| Output                                                                                        | Recipient                   |
| --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------- |
| **Decision letter** (approval, partial approval, or denial)                                   | Claimant                    |
| **Payment instruction** (approvals and partial approvals)                                     | Finance                     |
| **Audit record** (every check performed, supporting evidence, final decision, reviewer notes) | Compliance, internal review |

All communication with the claimant (acknowledgements, status updates, inspection notices, and decision letters) is delivered by email to the email address on file.

## 5. Process lifecycle

A claim moves through five primary stages. Two **secondary (conditional) stages** may pause the primary flow when additional information or activity is required from outside the Claims Department:

- **Awaiting Assessment**: the claim is held while an independent assessor inspects the property and produces the Professional Incident Report.
- **Details Inquiry**: the claim is held while additional information is being requested from the claimant.

A claim may pass through neither, one, or both secondary stages depending on the circumstances of the case.

## 7. Stage-by-stage procedure

### 7.1 Stage 1: Intake

**Purpose.** Receive the claim, capture the data from each document, and create the case record that will track the claim through the rest of the process.

**Entry.** A claim package arrives by online portal, email, or via an agent.

**Steps.**
1. Assign a unique claim reference number to the package.
2. Identify and classify each document — Claim Form, Insurance Policy, Professional Incident Report.
3. Capture the structured data from the Claim Form (claimant details, incident details, damage inventory, claim totals).
4. Retrieve the corresponding policy by policy number and attach it to the case.
5. Retrieve any prior claims associated with the claimant and attach them as reference data.
6. Send the claimant an email acknowledgement of receipt that includes the claim reference number.

**Exit.** Case record is created; documents are linked to the case; claimant has been acknowledged.

Notes:
- If the Professional Incident Report has not yet arrived, the claim can still proceed to **Eligibility Analysis**. The Incident Report is required from Stage 3 onward.
- If the **Claim Form** or **Insurance Policy** is missing, the claim enters the **Details Inquiry** secondary stage and the claimant is contacted to supply the missing document before any further processing.

---

### 7.2 Stage 2: Eligibility Analysis

**Purpose.** Confirm that the claim meets the threshold requirements for processing. No coverage analysis or payout calculation is performed at this stage.

**Entry.** Stage 1 is complete; at minimum the Claim Form and the Insurance Policy are on file.

**Steps.**
1. Verify that the policy is in force on the incident date (rule **E-1**).
2. Verify that the claimant is the named insured on the policy (rule **E-2**).
3. Verify that the property address on the claim matches the insured property address on the policy and, when available, the address on the Incident Report (rule **E-3**).
4. Verify that the incident date falls within the policy's effective and expiration dates (rule **E-4**).
5. Verify that the claim was filed within sixty days of the incident date. If the filing exceeds sixty days, check whether the claimant has provided a reasonable explanation (rule **E-5**).
6. Record the result of each check together with the source document and field that supports it.
7. Send the claimant an email update confirming that eligibility review is in progress.

**Exit.**

| Result | Next stage |
|---|---|
| All five checks pass | Proceed to Awaiting Assessment (if no Incident Report) or directly to Stage 3 (if the report is already on file) |
| Any hard failure (E-1, E-2, E-3, E-4) | Skip to Stage 5 with a denial decision |
| Late filing with credible explanation (E-5) | Escalate to a Senior Claims Adjuster |

**Senior Adjuster Review (escalation path).** The adjuster reviews the claim and chooses either **Continue** (the claim moves on to the next stage) or **Deny** (the claim moves to Stage 5 with a denial decision). The adjuster may attach review notes that are subsequently considered when drafting the decision letter. The adjuster may also invoke the **Details Inquiry** secondary stage if further information is required from the claimant before deciding.

---

### 7.3 Awaiting Assessment (Conditional)

**Purpose.** Pause the lifecycle while an independent licensed assessor inspects the property and produces the professional **Incident Report**. This stage runs only when the claim has cleared **Eligibility Analysis** and the report is not yet on file.

**Entry.** Eligibility Analysis confirmed; Professional Incident Report not yet received.

**Steps.**
1. Issue a request for inspection to the panel of licensed assessors or to the claimant's nominated assessor.
2. Confirm an inspection appointment with the claimant.
3. Send the claimant an email confirming the scheduled inspection date and the expected report timeline.
4. Await the assessor's report.
5. On receipt, attach the report to the case and send the claimant an email notifying them that the inspection is complete and analysis is resuming.

**Exit.** Professional Incident Report received and attached to the case. The claim returns to the main flow at Stage 3.

**Notes.**
- The standard inspection target is five business days from the date of the request. Delays beyond ten business days are flagged for follow-up.

---

### 7.4 Details Inquiry (Conditional)

**Purpose.** Pause the lifecycle while additional information is requested from the claimant. The stage handles situations where the claim cannot be progressed without further explanation, supporting documents, or clarification from the claimant.

**Entry.** Details Inquiry may be entered from any of the following points:

- **Intake** — when one of the required claim documents (Claim Form or Insurance Policy) is missing.
- **Eligibility Analysis** — when the claimant must clarify an eligibility detail, for example providing the explanation for a late filing (rule E-5).
- **Claim Review** — when the Senior Claims Adjuster determines that additional information is required before a final disposition can be issued.

**Steps.**
1. Identify the specific information required and the stage from which the inquiry was initiated.
2. Send an email to the claimant outlining what is needed and the response deadline.
3. Hold the claim in this stage.
4. On receipt of the claimant's reply, attach the supplementary information to the case and send the claimant an email confirming receipt.
5. Return the claim to the stage from which the inquiry was initiated and resume processing.

**Exit.** Either the claimant has responded with the requested information, or the response window has lapsed without a reply. If the response window lapses, the claim is referred to the Senior Claims Adjuster for a disposition based on the information already on file.

**Notes.**
- The standard response window is fourteen calendar days. A single reminder is sent on day seven.
- The claim's overall lifecycle clock continues to run while it is held in Details Inquiry; reviewers consider any apparent delay attributable to the claimant when assessing the timing dimension of the claim's credibility.

---

### 7.5 Stage 3: Data Analysis

**Purpose.** Determine whether the loss is covered, calculate the amount payable, assess the credibility of the claim, and arrive at a decision.

**Entry.** Eligibility confirmed; Professional Incident Report on file.

The analysis is performed in four parts.

#### 7.5.1 Assessment report validation

Before any substantive analysis the Professional Incident Report is validated against the claim under review:

- The report relates to the correct claim, property, and incident.
- The assessor's name, license number, and signature are present.
- The report is internally consistent (dates, cause description, damage observations, and estimate align).
- The report contains, at minimum, a cause determination, a damage inventory, and an itemised repair estimate.

If the report is materially unusable for analysis (wrong claim, missing cause determination, no estimate), the claim is escalated for a replacement report. If the report has minor gaps but is still usable, the analysis continues with the issues recorded.

#### 7.5.2 Coverage analysis

For each damage item in the claim:

1. Identify the peril responsible for the loss. Use the Incident Type from the Claim Form and the Cause Determination from the Incident Report. If they disagree, rely on the assessor's determination and record the discrepancy.
2. Assign the item to a coverage section — A (Dwelling), B (Other Structures), C (Personal Property), or D (Loss of Use).
3. Apply the open-peril rule for Coverages A and B: the loss is covered unless an exclusion in Section II of the policy applies. Walk through every relevant exclusion and explain, for this claim, whether each one does or does not apply (rule **C-1**).
4. Apply the named-peril rule for Coverage C: the peril must match one of the sixteen named perils listed in Section I, Coverage C of the policy (rule **C-2**).
5. Apply Coverage D when the claimant has incurred additional living expenses because of a covered loss.
6. Check every applicable exclusion specifically: flood and water damage (**C-3**), earth movement (**C-4**), gradual deterioration (**C-5**), neglect / failure to mitigate (**C-6**), vacancy clause (**C-7**), intentional loss (**C-8**).
7. Cite the exact policy section and language for every exclusion that applies or is considered.
8. Record the coverage result for each item: covered, excluded, or ambiguous.

#### 7.5.3 Payout calculation

For each item that has been determined to be covered:

1. Sum the claimed amounts by coverage section (rules **P-1** to **P-4**, capped by each section's limit of liability).
2. Apply sublimits to applicable categories of personal property — jewellery (**P-5**), electronics (**P-6**), and any other sublimited categories present in the policy schedule. Items are categorised by their nature, not by how the claimant labelled them.
3. Subtract the policy deductible once from the combined total of Coverages A, B, and C (rule **P-7**). The deductible does not apply to Coverage D.
4. Determine the settlement basis (rule **P-8**). When the Replacement Cost Endorsement is active, the loss is settled at replacement cost provided the claimant repairs or replaces the property. When the endorsement is absent or inactive, the loss is settled at Actual Cash Value (replacement cost less depreciation).
5. Cross-check the calculated payout against the independent repair estimate from the Incident Report (rule **P-9**). If the claimed amount is more than twenty per cent above the independent estimate, flag the claim for review. If it is more than fifty per cent above, escalate.

#### 7.5.4 Credibility assessment

Evaluate the claim along four dimensions and assign each a risk level of low, medium, or high:

| Dimension | Rule | What it checks |
|---|---|---|
| Narrative consistency | **CR-1** | Whether the claimant's description of what happened matches the assessor's findings. |
| Estimate reasonableness | **CR-2** | The ratio of the claimed amount to the independent repair estimate. |
| Documentation completeness | **CR-3** | Whether required signatures, license details, and required fields are present. |
| Timing and pattern | **CR-4** | The gap between incident, claim filing, and assessment, plus any pattern visible in the claimant's prior-claims history. |

A material contradiction with the assessor's cause determination (rule **CR-5**) is treated separately: the claim is escalated for review, and the assessor's determination is used for coverage analysis.

Credibility flags never amount to an accusation of fraud. They identify claims that require human judgement before a final decision is issued.

#### 7.5.5 Final decision

The results of the four analyses are reconciled into a single disposition using the priority rules in §8.5 and §9. The outcome is one of:

- **Approve** — proceed to Stage 5.
- **Partial approve** — proceed to Stage 5.
- **Deny** — proceed to Stage 5.
- **Escalate** — proceed to Stage 4.

Send the claimant an email update confirming that analysis is complete and that a decision is forthcoming.

---

### 7.6 Stage 4: Claim Review

**Purpose.** Apply senior human judgement to claims that the Data Analysis stage was unable to decide with confidence.

**Entry.** Stage 3 returned a decision of `escalate`.

**Steps.**
1. The case is assigned to a Senior Claims Adjuster.
2. The adjuster reviews all three documents, every check performed in Stage 3, the calculated payout, and the credibility findings.
3. If additional information is required, the adjuster invokes the **Details Inquiry** secondary stage and waits for the claimant's response before deciding.
4. The adjuster issues a final disposition: **Continue** (proceed with the calculated outcome — approval, partial approval, or as otherwise indicated) or **Deny**.
5. The adjuster may record review notes describing the reasoning and any additional information considered.

**Authority.** The Senior Claims Adjuster has full discretion. The adjuster may continue a claim that the analysis recommended denying, or deny a claim that the analysis recommended approving. The adjuster's decision is final.

**Exit.** Final disposition recorded; the claim proceeds to Stage 5.

---

### 7.7 Stage 5: Settlement and Closure

**Purpose.** Communicate the decision to the claimant, disburse any payment due, and close the case.

**Entry.** A final decision has been reached, either automatically at the end of Stage 3 or by a Senior Claims Adjuster at the end of Stage 4.

**Steps.**
1. Draft the decision letter following the structure and tone in §11. Approvals and partial approvals include the itemised payout breakdown; denials and partial denials cite the specific policy section that supports the denial; all letters include appeal rights.
2. Send the decision letter to the claimant by email to the address on file.
3. For approvals and partial approvals, issue the payment instruction to Finance.
4. Update the case status to closed and archive the complete case record for audit purposes.
5. Send the claimant a final email confirmation that the case has been closed and provide the reference number for any future correspondence.

**Exit.** Email sent; payment instruction issued (if applicable); case archived.

## 8. Validation rules

Every check performed in the process has a stable identifier. These identifiers appear on the audit record and may be cited in decision letters.

### 8.1 Eligibility rules

| ID      | Rule                                        | Pass condition                                                                                                            | On failure                                                                |
| ------- | ------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **E-1** | Policy in force on the incident date        | Payment status is *Current* or *Paid*                                                                                     | Deny: "Policy not in force on the incident date"                          |
| **E-2** | Claimant identity matches the named insured | Names refer to the same person (minor variations such as nicknames are acceptable)                                        | Deny: "Claimant is not the named insured, please resubmit"                |
| **E-3** | Property address matches                    | The address on all three documents resolves to the same physical address after normalisation                              | Deny: "Claimed property does not match insured property, please resubmit" |
| **E-4** | Incident within the policy period           | Incident date falls between the effective and expiration dates of the policy                                              | Deny: "Loss occurred outside the policy period"                           |
| **E-5** | Filing deadline                             | Claim filed within sixty calendar days of the incident, or the claimant has provided a credible explanation for the delay | Deny if no explanation provided; otherwise escalate                       |

### 8.2 Coverage rules

| ID | Rule | Logic |
|---|---|---|
| **C-1** | Coverage A and B open-peril gate | A loss to the dwelling or other structures is covered unless an exclusion in Section II of the policy applies. Each exclusion is considered individually and the reasoning is recorded. |
| **C-2** | Coverage C named-peril gate | A loss to personal property is covered only if the peril matches one of the sixteen named perils listed in Section I, Coverage C. |
| **C-3** | Flood and water exclusion | Excludes flood, surface water, sewer backup, groundwater, and any water below the surface of the ground. Wind-driven rain entering through a wind-damaged opening is covered as part of the wind loss. |
| **C-4** | Earth movement exclusion | Excludes earthquake, landslide, mudflow, and earth shifting. |
| **C-5** | Gradual deterioration exclusion | Excludes wear and tear, rot, gradual seepage, mechanical breakdown, and similar non-sudden causes. |
| **C-6** | Neglect / duty to mitigate | If the claimant fails to take reasonable steps to prevent further damage, the payout is reduced or the claim is denied. |
| **C-7** | Vacancy clause | If the property has been vacant for more than sixty consecutive days before the loss, vandalism, theft, and water-damage claims are excluded; all other claims are reduced by fifteen per cent. |
| **C-8** | Intentional loss exclusion | Excludes any loss caused by an intentional act of an insured. |

### 8.3 Payout rules

| ID      | Rule                 | Logic                                                                                                                                                                                               |
| ------- | -------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **P-1** | Coverage A cap       | The payout under Coverage A cannot exceed the Dwelling limit of liability.                                                                                                                          |
| **P-2** | Coverage B cap       | The payout under Coverage B cannot exceed the Other Structures limit of liability.                                                                                                                  |
| **P-3** | Coverage C cap       | The payout under Coverage C, after sublimits, cannot exceed the Personal Property limit of liability.                                                                                               |
| **P-4** | Coverage D cap       | The payout under Coverage D cannot exceed the Loss of Use limit of liability.                                                                                                                       |
| **P-5** | Jewellery sublimit   | The aggregate payout for jewellery, watches, furs, and precious stones is capped at the jewellery sublimit.                                                                                         |
| **P-6** | Electronics sublimit | The aggregate payout for electronics, computers, and related equipment is capped at the electronics sublimit.                                                                                       |
| **P-7** | Deductible           | The deductible is subtracted once from the combined total of Coverages A, B, and C. It does not apply to Coverage D.                                                                                |
| **P-8** | Settlement basis     | When the Replacement Cost Endorsement is active, settle at replacement cost provided the property is repaired or replaced. When the endorsement is absent or inactive, settle at Actual Cash Value. |
| **P-9** | Reasonableness check | When the claimed amount exceeds the independent repair estimate by more than twenty per cent, flag the claim for review. When it exceeds by more than fifty per cent, escalate.                     |

### 8.4 Credibility rules

| ID       | Rule                                                | Risk level guide                                                                                                                                                                                                               |
| -------- | --------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **CR-1** | Narrative consistency between claimant and assessor | Material contradiction → high. Minor wording differences → low.                                                                                                                                                                |
| **CR-2** | Estimate reasonableness                             | Same ratio as P-9. 1.00–1.20 → low. 1.21–1.50 → medium. Greater than 1.50 → high.                                                                                                                                              |
| **CR-3** | Documentation completeness                          | Missing signature, unsigned report, blank required fields → medium or high depending on which fields.                                                                                                                          |
| **CR-4** | Timing and pattern                                  | Incident-to-claim gap greater than thirty days is notable; greater than forty-five days is a flag. Incident-to-assessment gap greater than sixty days is notable. A prior-claims pattern of repeated similar incidents → high. |
| **CR-5** | Cause mismatch between claimant and assessor        | Escalate; rely on the assessor's determination for coverage analysis.                                                                                                                                                          |

### 8.5 Final-decision rules

| ID | Rule | Condition | Decision |
|---|---|---|---|
| **D-1** | Hard denial | Any hard eligibility failure (E-1, E-2, E-3, E-4 without justification); or no item is covered under any section; or all claimed items are excluded by the policy | Deny |
| **D-2** | Escalation | Any of: filing was late with justification; cause of loss is ambiguous (C-1 vs. C-3, mixed perils); reasonableness check flagged (P-9); credibility risk is high; documentation gaps prevent calculation | Escalate |
| **D-3** | Partial approval | Some claimed items are covered and some are excluded; no escalation flag | Partial approve |
| **D-4** | Approval | Eligibility passes; coverage is complete; no escalation flag; reasonableness check passes | Approve |

The rules are applied in strict priority order: **D-1 takes priority over D-2; D-2 takes priority over D-3; D-3 takes priority over D-4**. A denial condition overrides any escalation; an escalation condition overrides any approval.

## 9. Decision matrix

```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  Does any hard-denial condition apply (D-1)?                     │
│    YES → decision = Deny                                         │
│    NO  ▼                                                         │
│  Does any escalation condition apply (D-2)?                      │
│    YES → decision = Escalate → Senior Adjuster Review            │
│    NO  ▼                                                         │
│  Is coverage mixed — some items covered, some excluded (D-3)?    │
│    YES → decision = Partial Approve                              │
│    NO  ▼                                                         │
│  decision = Approve (D-4)                                        │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

A Senior Claims Adjuster may, at any escalation point, continue a claim that the analysis recommended denying or deny a claim that the analysis recommended approving. The adjuster's decision is final and is recorded on the audit trail.

## 10. Common claim issues

The catalogue below lists the most frequent adverse findings the Claims Department encounters and how each is handled.

| Issue | Typical cause | Process response |
|---|---|---|
| Policy not in force | Premium lapsed before the incident; policy cancelled; policy expired | Deny (E-1) |
| Claimant is not the named insured | Claim filed by a non-policyholder occupant, dependent, or unrelated party | Deny (E-2) |
| Address mismatch | Loss occurred at a different property than the one insured under the policy | Deny (E-3) |
| Incident outside the policy period | Loss occurred before the policy effective date or after expiration | Deny (E-4) |
| Late filing | Claim filed more than sixty days after the incident | Deny if no justification; escalate if justification is provided (E-5) |
| Excluded peril — flood | Damage caused by surface water, storm surge, sewer backup, or groundwater | Deny (C-3) |
| Excluded peril — earthquake | Damage caused by ground movement | Deny (C-4) |
| Gradual deterioration | Long-term seepage, rot, mould caused by slow leak, wear and tear | Deny (C-5) |
| Failure to mitigate | Claimant did not take reasonable steps to prevent additional damage | Reduce or deny (C-6) |
| Vacancy | Property unoccupied for more than sixty consecutive days before the loss | Exclude vandalism / theft / water; reduce other claims by 15 % (C-7) |
| Intentional damage | Evidence that the loss was caused deliberately by an insured | Deny (C-8) |
| Amount exceeds coverage limit | Claim larger than the section's limit of liability | Cap at the limit (P-1 to P-4) |
| Sublimit breach | High-value jewellery or electronics exceed the category sublimit | Cap at the sublimit (P-5, P-6) |
| Inflated estimate | Claimed amount significantly higher than the independent estimate | Flag for review (>20 %) or escalate (>50 %) (P-9 / CR-2) |
| Narrative inconsistency | Claimant's description contradicts the assessor's findings | Escalate (CR-1) |
| Cause mismatch | Claimant-reported cause differs from the assessor's determination | Escalate; rely on assessor (CR-5) |
| Missing documentation | Unsigned forms or report, blank required fields, no license number | Hold or escalate (CR-3) |
| Mixed cause (wind and flood) | Storm damage with both wind and flood components | Partial approve — cover wind portion, deny flood portion |
| Pattern in prior claims | Repeated claims of the same type from the same claimant | Escalate (CR-4) |

## 11. Communication guidelines

The decision letter is the primary communication to the claimant and must follow the standards below. All claimant communications — acknowledgements, status updates, inspection notices, and decision letters — are delivered by email to the address on file. Letters are written in the language appropriate to the claimant's locale, determined from the claimant's address; the claim reference number and policy number appear on every letter.

### 11.1 Approval letter

- Open with the favourable outcome: *"We are pleased to inform you that your claim has been approved."*
- State the net payout amount and currency.
- Provide an itemised breakdown by coverage section, including any sublimits applied and the deductible.
- When the Replacement Cost Endorsement applies, explain that the initial payment is at Actual Cash Value and the balance will be paid upon proof of repair or replacement.
- State the payment timeline (typically within thirty business days).
- Include contact information for the Claims Department.
- Tone: professional, warm, clear.

### 11.2 Partial-approval letter

- Acknowledge the claim and state that it has been partially approved.
- Clearly separate the approved portion from the denied portion.
- For the approved portion, provide the same detail as a full approval.
- For the denied portion, cite the specific policy section and language that supports the denial.
- Include appeal rights.
- Tone: balanced, transparent.

### 11.3 Denial letter

- Open directly but with empathy: *"After careful review of your claim, we are unable to provide coverage for this loss."*
- State the specific reason, citing the policy section and language verbatim. Avoid vague language such as "not covered."
- Inform the claimant that they have sixty days to submit a written appeal and may also request appraisal under the policy's appraisal provision.
- Provide contact information.
- Tone: professional, respectful, factual.

### 11.4 Common requirements (all letters)

- The header contains the claim reference number, policy number, claimant name and address, and the date.
- The closing reads: *"If you have questions about this determination, please contact our Claims Department or your agent at the contact information on your policy declarations page."*
- Internal terminology (rule identifiers, risk levels, internal scoring) is never exposed to the claimant.
- When notes from a Senior Claims Adjuster are relevant to the outcome, the substance of those notes is incorporated naturally without attribution.

## 12. Special situations

### 12.1 Water-damage ambiguity

Water claims are the most common source of dispute. A sudden burst pipe is a covered peril. A slow leak that causes mould over months is gradual deterioration and is excluded. Sewer backup is excluded unless a specific endorsement applies. Flood is always excluded under HO-3. When the cause involves water, the assessor's cause determination governs. *Sudden pipe burst* is covered; *long-term seepage* is excluded.

### 12.2 Wind and flood in the same storm

During hurricanes and severe storms both wind and flood damage may be present. Wind damage to the roof is covered. Flooding from storm surge is excluded. The Incident Report must distinguish between the two. If it does, the claim is partially approved (wind covered, flood excluded). If it does not, the claim is escalated.

### 12.3 Vacancy

A claimant who was away on vacation for three weeks does not render the property vacant under the policy. Vacancy refers to properties unoccupied and unfurnished for extended periods (for example, between tenants, or during prolonged travel exceeding the sixty-day threshold). When in doubt, escalate.

### 12.4 Late filing with good cause

When a claim is filed more than sixty days after the incident but the claimant was hospitalised, deployed overseas, or otherwise unable to file, this may constitute reasonable cause. The claim is escalated rather than denied automatically. A Senior Claims Adjuster makes the final judgement.

### 12.5 Sublimit disputes

When a claimant lists a high-value item under a general category — for example a $10,000 watch listed under "general personal property" rather than as jewellery — the sublimit still applies. Items are categorised by their nature, not by the claimant's labelling.

### 12.6 Replacement cost versus actual cash value

When the Replacement Cost Endorsement is active, the claimant is entitled to the full replacement cost — but only if the item is actually repaired or replaced. The initial payment is issued at Actual Cash Value; the remainder is paid upon proof of repair or replacement. If the claimant elects not to repair or replace, settlement reverts to Actual Cash Value. When the policy does not carry the Replacement Cost Endorsement, settlement is always at Actual Cash Value.

### 12.7 Repeated claims

When a claimant has filed multiple prior claims for the same type of incident — particularly within a short window — the case is escalated for senior review regardless of how clean the current claim appears in isolation.

## 13. Glossary

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **FNOL** | First Notice of Loss — the claimant's initial submission reporting a loss |
| **HO-3** | "Homeowners 3 — Special Form" — open-peril dwelling coverage with named-peril personal-property coverage |
| **Coverage A** | Dwelling — the main residence and structures attached to it |
| **Coverage B** | Other Structures — detached structures on the premises (garages, sheds, fences) |
| **Coverage C** | Personal Property — the insured's belongings |
| **Coverage D** | Loss of Use — additional living expenses incurred when the insured is displaced |
| **Sublimit** | A category-specific cap inside the overall Coverage C limit (for example a jewellery sublimit) |
| **Deductible** | The amount the claimant bears before the policy pays |
| **Replacement Cost** | The cost of replacing the damaged item with a new one of similar kind and quality |
| **Actual Cash Value (ACV)** | Replacement Cost less depreciation for age and wear |
| **Replacement Cost Endorsement** | Optional addition that settles losses at replacement cost provided the property is actually repaired or replaced |
| **Vacancy Clause** | Policy condition reducing or excluding coverage when the property has been vacant for more than sixty days |
| **Duty to Mitigate** | Claimant's obligation to take reasonable steps to prevent further damage |
| **Named Peril** | A peril listed explicitly in the policy as covered |
| **Open Peril** | Coverage that applies to any cause of loss unless specifically excluded |
| **Independent Estimate** | The repair estimate produced by the licensed assessor in the Incident Report |
| **Audit Record** | The complete trail of checks, evidence, and decisions preserved for each claim |
